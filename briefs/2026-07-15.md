*A fifth straight quiet week for the core STT/TTS vendors, but two threads worth pulling: a TTS challenger that's winning on price *and* quality at once — a combination the last four leaderboard flips haven't managed — and OpenAI attaching the word "AGI" to a voice feature for the first time, which raises the bar for what "sounds natural" is now supposed to prove.*

## Where the puck is going

**TTS leaderboard churn just picked up a new axis.** This brief has tracked three different #1 TTS models in four weeks (Alibaba Fun-Realtime-TTS, Grok TTS on Vapi, MiniMax Speech 2.8) — each winning on a quality metric alone, at roughly comparable price points. Speechify's Simba 3.2 breaks that pattern: #1 on quality *and* the cheapest model in the top ten by a wide margin. If price-performance becomes a leaderboard-topping strategy rather than just a mid-tier commoditization story, it's a more direct threat to the ElevenLabs/Cartesia session-stack bet — those companies have been pricing on bundled orchestration value, not raw per-character cost, and a genuinely cheap #1 model narrows that gap. Worth watching whether Speechify tries to extend past its reading-app roots into the session-orchestration layer, or stays a component play.

**Consumer voice-tuning dials keep expanding in reach, not just in number.** Apple's Siri pace/expressivity sliders moved from a narrow developer beta (flagged here July 14) to the full public beta one day later — still hardware-gated, but visible to a much larger test pool. This is the same "production-tuning-dial" pattern tracked since June 30 (ElevenLabs' `interruption_ignore_terms`, Deepgram's mid-session threshold updates) now scaling through Apple's release rings toward general availability, rather than staying inside a small beta cohort.

**OpenAI raising the rhetorical stakes on voice naturalism is worth treating skeptically, not dismissively.** Pairing "sounds human" with "a step toward AGI" is a marketing choice — AGI has no agreed technical definition, and full-duplex backchanneling is a UX feature, not a reasoning benchmark. But the framing matters for builders: OpenAI is signaling it now treats conversational naturalness as core to its intelligence narrative, not a side feature, which likely means faster internal investment in exactly the interruption/backchannel tuning this brief has tracked since May — and it sits uncomfortably close to the emotional-reliance risk OpenAI itself flagged for GPT-Live on July 9. Expect more scrutiny of that pairing as GPT-Live's install base grows.
