## Where the puck is going

**The STT benchmark conversation just changed format.** Smallest AI's week-long Pulse campaign (June 1–4) ran the same evaluations on raw non-normalized audio, under heavy noise, and on real-world call datasets — AMI meetings, Earnings22 financial transcripts, SPGISpeech compliance recordings. The gap it exposes is real: on raw audio Grok STT degrades from 7.6% to 60% WER; Deepgram from 6.6% to 11.9%; Pulse holds at 6.0%. Under heavy noise the spread widens to 13 points. For builders deploying in contact centers, retail, or noisy field environments, the headline benchmark WER was always a controlled-conditions artifact. Smallest AI is the first vendor to campaign on production-realistic evaluation consistently — which forces every competitor to defend their real-condition numbers or cede the narrative. Expect Deepgram and AssemblyAI to respond with their own condition-specific data; the era of single-number WER comparisons is ending.

**ElevenLabs is repositioning from TTS vendor to voice session infrastructure.** Speech Engine isn't a TTS product — it's a "bring your own LLM, we own the voice session" play. The architecture is deliberate: ElevenLabs controls STT, turn-taking, and TTS inside a WebSocket connection; the developer's server handles agent logic and streams response text back. Combined with the quality-vs-latency fork documented last week (v3 for studio, Flash v2.5 for real-time), ElevenLabs now covers three tiers: expressive TTS, real-time TTS, and full voice session management. The strategic logic: own the voice interface to the tens of millions of LLM-based agents already in production that don't yet have a voice layer. This is a moat play — BYOL architecture means ElevenLabs isn't competing with OpenAI on the LLM, it's inserting itself as the mandatory voice plumbing layer around any LLM.

**Compliance is becoming the moat that latency benchmarks can't substitute for.** Deepgram's confidential-computing partnership answers a specific enterprise objection that cloud-API pricing never solves: "I cannot send patient audio or financial conversations to a third-party endpoint." Hardware-level TEE with encrypted audio and model weights during inference is a real procurement differentiator — not just SOC2 paperwork. As voice agents move from demo to production in regulated verticals, data sovereignty is hardening into a named criterion. The companies with a credible on-prem answer will close deals the cloud-only stack can't touch.
