## Where the puck is going

**Voice UX tuning is now visibly a consumer-platform problem, not just a developer-API one.** This brief has tracked the shift from raw model jumps to tunable production dials since late June — ElevenLabs' `interruption_ignore_terms`, Deepgram's mid-session threshold updates. Apple's Siri sliders for pace and expressivity are the first time that pattern has shipped as an end-user-facing control on a mass consumer assistant, not a builder-facing API parameter. OpenAI's GPT-Live sits on the other side of the same coin: it shipped *without* an equivalent dial for backchannel frequency, and six days later still doesn't have one. The lesson for builders is blunt — ship the tuning knob alongside the naturalism feature, not after the complaints arrive, especially on anything full-duplex.

**OpenAI's fix cadence is uneven depending on what kind of problem it is.** Tool-call dead air — an architectural fix (route to background reasoning) — shipped inside gpt-realtime-2.1-mini within days of being named a problem. Backchanneling frequency — a UX-tuning problem that needs real usage data to calibrate — is still unaddressed after a week. API access for GPT-Live, flagged as a gap on day one, took a week to even get a signup form. Different problem types are moving at different speeds inside the same team, which is a useful signal for anyone estimating how fast a platform vendor will respond to a specific class of production complaint.

**The infra-layer quiet spell is now structural, not a blip.** Four straight weeks without a Cartesia/Deepgram/AssemblyAI/Speechmatics/Soniox launch, following a spring and early summer that produced a new leaderboard leader almost weekly, suggests the model-quality race has genuinely slowed — consistent with this brief's ongoing thesis that defensible value has moved to session orchestration, ops tooling, and now platform-level UX polish rather than raw STT/TTS benchmarks.
