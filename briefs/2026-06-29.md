## Where the puck is going

**The rollback rate is the number that should terrify — and instruct.** Sinch surveyed 2,527 enterprise decision-makers and found 74% have already reversed a live AI agent deployment. That's not the demo-to-production gap (60%, per the June 23 data) — it's a *production-to-rollback* gap, one step further down the funnel. The counterintuitive finding is the real insight: organizations with the most mature guardrails roll back at 81%, *higher* than the average, because better governance detects failures faster. The winning architecture isn't "deploy perfectly" — it's "detect and recover faster than anyone else." This validates the recovery-loop thesis (June 1), the Waymo testing analogy (June 25), and Coval's $28M bet: the moat is observability speed, not deployment quality.

**STT is fragmenting into specialists, and the Pareto frontier is getting crowded.** Three new STT models in recent weeks — Soniox v5 (price-performance), Speechmatics Melia (multilingual code-switching), Cartesia Ink-2 (raw accuracy + semantic turn detection) — each optimize for a different edge of the accuracy-latency-price-language triangle. Soniox v5 costs $2/1K min; ElevenLabs Scribe v2 Realtime costs roughly 4x more for ~1 WER point better. Speechmatics Melia handles 55+ language code-switching natively — the capability ServiceNow's benchmark (June 15) showed monolingual models fail at by 30–50% WER. Add Gladia Solaria-3 (European business audio), Gnani Prisma v2.5 (Indic), and Smallest AI Pulse (East Asian) from recent weeks, and STT procurement is now a multi-axis vendor selection. Aggregate WER leaderboards are actively misleading; the right model depends on your language mix, deployment surface, and latency budget.
