## Where the puck is going

**Sovereign investment in voice AI is a new signal class.** Poland's $11M stake in ElevenLabs is small relative to the company's $11B valuation, but the signal is outsized: a nation-state is now equity-invested in voice AI infrastructure. This follows Apple's ~$1B/year Gemini deal (June 8) — governments and trillion-dollar platforms alike are treating voice AI as strategic infrastructure, not a feature experiment. For builders, two implications: the regulatory and compliance environment around voice AI will tighten as governments develop direct financial stakes in the ecosystem, and geographic talent hubs for voice AI are crystallizing (Warsaw joining London, SF, Seoul). ElevenLabs' $500M ARR — adding $150M in recurring revenue in 120 days — is the economic validation. This is a growth-stage company being treated as national infrastructure. The gap between ElevenLabs' revenue trajectory and every other pure-play voice AI company is widening: Deepgram raised $130M at $1.3B in January; ElevenLabs now generates $500M ARR at $11B. The market is pricing in that the voice session layer (STT + TTS + orchestration) is winner-take-most at the platform level.

**The STT competitive map keeps fragmenting below the headline providers.** Gladia's Solaria-3 is the third STT challenger to surface in recent weeks, after Soniox's Tencent Cloud integration (June 8) and NVIDIA's Nemotron 3.5 ASR open-source release (June 11). Each occupies a different niche: Gladia targets European business audio, Soniox targets multilingual telephony, and Nemotron targets self-hosted fine-tunable deployments. The pattern is clear: production STT is fragmenting by vertical, language mix, and deployment model. Aggregate WER leaderboards are becoming less useful for procurement decisions. Gladia's Earnings22 results (6.4% WER vs Deepgram's 12.0%) demonstrate that a 2x gap can exist on business audio even between vendors that look comparable on aggregate leaderboards. This connects directly to the ServiceNow code-switching finding (June 15): benchmark on your specific audio type, not on published leaderboards.

Driven by Speech Engine adoption, Scribe v2, and 75% Fortune 500 penetration. - **Gladia on STT methodology (blog, June 10):** *"Deepgram mangles the company name and writes all numbers as words — the kind of error that makes downstream parsing unreliable on every financial call."* — Highlights how aggregate WER underweights semantic errors (wrong entity names, wrong number formats) that break downstream automations. ([Gladia](https://www.gladia.io/solaria-3))
- **Industry data (multiple sources, June 2026):** Enterprise voice AI adoption at 97%, but only 12% satisfaction with deployments. The gap maps to the eval/testing maturity problem surfaced by Coval/Pipecat last cycle: companies can deploy voice agents but can't reliably validate whether they're working.
