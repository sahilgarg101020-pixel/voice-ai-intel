## Where the puck is going

**Investors are pricing the full-stack voice session layer, not the model.** ElevenLabs' valuation doubling in five months lands right after the company finished assembling its session stack — Scribe v2 STT, Speech Engine, Flash TTS, ElevenAgents telephony — the exact "own the whole call" bet this brief has tracked since early June. TTS model quality itself has been unstable at the top for weeks (three different #1 leaderboard models in a month), so a valuation move this size isn't a bet on ElevenLabs having the best voice — it's a bet that owning the orchestration, telephony, and enterprise ops layer around voice is the durable business, exactly as the "session-stack race" theme has argued. If you're building a point solution (just TTS, just STT), this is the clearest signal yet of what multiple compression looks like versus what commands a premium.

**Voice-as-input is becoming default assistant furniture, not a product.** Gemini Spark's Ramble mode is the same move Apple, Anthropic, and Microsoft have each made this year: bolt a press-and-hold voice capture onto an existing agentic/productivity surface rather than sell voice as the product. That's now four major platforms (Apple Siri/Campo, Anthropic Claude voice, Microsoft Copilot/Voice Live, Google Spark) treating voice input as a checkbox feature on top of a text-first agent. For founders: dictation-and-draft voice UX is now table stakes at the platform level and not a wedge worth building a company around — the open ground is still structured, tool-calling phone/call-center agents, which none of these four have shipped as a consumer-facing product.

**Quiet weeks are informative too.** This is the second brief in a row (July 1, now July 3) where AIEWF's own Voice & Realtime track — a 6,000-engineer conference explicitly billed as voice's developer coming-out party — has produced no confirmed on-stage product launch in public coverage. Combined with two straight weeks of no fresh word from Cartesia, Deepgram, AssemblyAI, or Speechmatics, the pace of weekly step-function announcements from the STT/TTS layer that defined May–June appears to be cooling, consistent with the "the model-level advantage doesn't last" theme — vendors may be shifting effort toward the harder, slower-to-announce work (ops, reliability, enterprise integration) rather than headline benchmarks.
