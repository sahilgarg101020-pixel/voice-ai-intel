## Where the Puck Is Going

**Architecture is becoming the differentiator.** Thinking Machines Lab's framing is the most architecturally ambitious stake in the ground since GPT-Realtime launched. The core argument: current real-time voice (including OpenAI's) bolts interactivity onto a model built for sequential reasoning. TML-Interaction-Small instead treats interactivity as a first-class architectural primitive — the system processes audio, video, and text simultaneously in 200ms micro-turns, enabling genuine full-duplex behavior across interruptions, backchannels, and overlapping speech. The FD-bench v1.5 lead (77.8 vs 46.8 for GPT-Realtime-2 and 45.5 for Gemini 3.1 Flash Live) and turn latency (0.40s vs 1.18s for GPT-Realtime-2) suggest this isn't positioning noise. For a builder, the implication is that the competitive axis is shifting from "what's your TTFB?" to "how natural is the full conversation across a chaotic real phone call?" — and the existing leaders are structurally behind on that test.

**Unexpected price pressure.** xAI's $3/hr Grok Voice is getting named explicitly in developer conversations today, positioned directly against OpenAI Realtime-2's higher price point. The gap is large enough to shift build vs buy math for cost-sensitive workloads. More consequentially, the Starlink production metrics (70% containment, 20% conversion, 28 tools across hundreds of workflows) are the kind of third-party validation that moves enterprise procurement. As xAI's distribution through Starlink/SpaceX reinforces the production story, this becomes a forcing function on OpenAI and others to justify the premium — or cut.

**Full-stack bundling is the new developer default.** LiveKit surfacing a complete xAI pipeline (STT + LLM + TTS) under a single key directly mirrors what Deepgram and AssemblyAI have built with their own stacks. The consistent pattern: orchestration layers (LiveKit, Vapi) are the assembly point, and every STT/TTS vendor is racing to offer a frictionless full-stack bundle to own the developer relationship. Raw model quality alone won't hold a customer — price/performance and integration convenience at scale is where the decision actually happens. This theme (first noted May 30) is hardening fast.
