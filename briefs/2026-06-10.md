## Where the puck is going

**TTS commoditization is arriving faster than most builders priced in.** Alibaba hitting #1 on the Artificial Analysis Speech Arena isn't just a benchmark — it signals that hyperscalers with massive compute advantages (Alibaba Cloud, Google Gemini Live, xAI) are now directly competitive with specialist pure-play TTS vendors at lower price points. $27.6/1M chars at the top of the human-preference leaderboard is corrosive to anyone pricing above it on quality alone. Combined with the sustained open-source cadence (Higgs Audio v3, Miso One, MOSS-TTS-Nano in the same week), the floor on mid-tier TTS is collapsing. The directional bet for builders: TTS is a rounding error in your COGS within 18 months. The defensible layer is the session, the turn-taking logic, the recovery loop, and the orchestration — not the speech synthesis bytes.

**The session-stack race is crystallizing into two distinct approaches.** ElevenLabs completed its stack last week: Scribe v2 Realtime STT + Speech Engine turn-taking + Flash v2.5 TTS + ElevenAgents telephony — a bundle-and-lock play where switching cost compounds across layers. Cartesia's answer with Ink-2 is performance-first: #1 on streaming STT accuracy, semantic endpointing that understands turn *meaning* rather than silence gaps, and an eager_end signal that hands the LLM the transcript before the speaker has actually stopped — shaving latency at the turn boundary. These are materially different bets on where the moat lives: ElevenLabs says it's the session abstraction and the developer workflow; Cartesia says it's the raw inference loop. OpenAI Realtime and Gemini Live are the wildcards — pure speech-to-speech architectures that skip the pipeline entirely. All four are converging on the same prize: the mandatory voice plumbing layer around every LLM in production.

**Benchmark literacy is now a required builder skill.** Artificial Analysis has launched three distinct voice evaluation frameworks in roughly two weeks: AA-WER v2.0 agent-specific (June 2), the Speech Arena TTS quality leaderboard (ongoing), and now AA-WER Streaming for streaming STT. A vendor at #1 on one may rank poorly on another — Cartesia leads streaming STT; ElevenLabs Scribe v2 leads agent-specific transcription accuracy; Alibaba leads human-preference TTS quality. For procurement decisions, the right question is no longer "who's #1?" but "which benchmark matches my production scenario?"
