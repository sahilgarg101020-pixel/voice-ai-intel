## Insights
The money and the moat are visibly shifting **up the stack**. Vapi and LiveKit (a $1B orchestration layer, Jan) winning at enterprise scale signals buyers are paying for the agent-orchestration and reliability layer, not raw TTS/STT — which is commoditizing fast. Amazon Ring evaluating 40+ vendors before standardizing on one is a preview of how large enterprises will consolidate.

On the model layer, the battleground is **latency + turn-taking**, not voice quality. Deepgram Flux (end-of-speech detection) and AssemblyAI (sub-310ms streaming) are competing on who can make agents feel non-awkward in real conversation — the same instinct behind Cartesia's sub-100ms Sonic positioning. OpenAI's GPT-Realtime-2 family collapses the STT→LLM→TTS chain entirely, which is the structural threat to pure-play STT/TTS vendors over the next year.

Funding data backs this: ~$2.58B across 33 conversational-AI companies in the trailing year, with voice platforms taking 50% of deal count. Capital is flowing to platforms and orchestration, not point models.

**Trend to watch (baseline, day 1):** the split between (a) infra/orchestration plays scaling into enterprise revenue and (b) consumer apps (Sesame) testing whether voice can be a destination, not just a feature. Next briefs should track whether Cartesia, Smallest AI, PlayHT and Resemble counter the latency leaders, and whether OpenAI's realtime stack starts pulling logos away from specialist STT/TTS vendors.
