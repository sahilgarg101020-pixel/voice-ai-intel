## Where the puck is going

**Function calling is the new latency race.** Last cycle we named retrieval speed as the voice-agent bottleneck. The OpenAI GA release reframes the competition: the bottleneck is now *tool call quality* — does the agent call the right function at the right moment with correct arguments? OpenAI's ComplexFuncBench (audio-native) and the 17-point improvement over the previous model is the first time this has been formally measured in an end-to-end realtime context. Builders layering tool integrations should benchmark against their actual tool surfaces — aggregate latency metrics alone will mislead you.

**Async tool calling quietly breaks the old architecture.** GPT-Realtime-2's ability to keep the conversation alive ("let me check that…") while a backend call resolves means the frozen-silence gap — historically patched by filler-phrase middleware and complex turn-management — is now a model-layer feature. That compresses the value of a class of orchestration middleware. For Vapi and LiveKit, the defensible surface shifts further toward reliability, compliance, routing, and multi-provider fallback — not turn management.

**Platform gravity is accelerating fast.** Google threading voice through audio glasses hardware, the Docs Live ambient transcription feature, and Gemini Spark means voice input is transitioning from opt-in to the default modality in Google's entire product suite. The mid-tier consumer voice app (think: single-purpose voice journaling, voice productivity, AI companion) gets harder to defend as Google ships these natively. Builders here need a clear answer to "why not just use the Google one?" before they raise or scale.

**Cartesia's SSM bet is paying architecture dividends.** Sonic 3.5's State Space Model scales inference costs linearly vs the quadratic cost of transformer stacks — which means as model size grows, Cartesia's unit economics improve faster than transformer-based competitors. At 40ms TTFA, it is now below the perceptual threshold for most turn types. The Sonic 2 deprecation on June 1 is a forcing function; any production team still on the old model needs to migrate this week.

## Signals from the field

**Developer community on GPT-Realtime-2 GA** (OpenAI Dev Forum, May 11): The async tool calling feature dominated discussion — builders cited eliminating the frozen-silence problem as the single highest-impact change, with several noting it made a "complete architectural rethink" of their agent flows worthwhile. ([forum](https://community.openai.com/t/new-realtime-voice-models-in-the-api/1380471))

**Trust infrastructure is not a nice-to-have**: FBI reports $893M in AI-voice-related scam losses in 2025; CNN (May 29, 2026) quotes security experts that voice replicas "may no longer" carry any detectable tells. Builders deploying voice in healthcare, financial services, or any authenticated flow who don't have a liveness detection answer are building on an exposed surface.

**Quiq's cross-channel continuity bet**: Quiq's positioning — retain full conversation context when a customer moves from messaging to live voice mid-ticket — is a concrete signal that enterprise buyers are demanding omnichannel memory, not just voice quality. This is a product requirement most voice-only vendors haven't shipped. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/11/quiq-extends-ai-agent-platform-voice-enterprise-rollouts-move-past-pilots/))
