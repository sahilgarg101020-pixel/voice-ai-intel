## Where the puck is going

**Real-time translation is commoditizing inside the voice stack, faster than most builders priced in.** Six weeks ago, live S2S translation was a specialty product — expensive interpreter APIs or heavy on-prem ASR+MT+TTS pipelines. OpenAI launched GPT-Realtime-Translate on May 7 ($0.034/min, 70+ input/13 output languages). Google replied June 9 with Gemini 3.5 Live Translate at $0.023/min — a 33% undercut with broader language auto-detection and explicit intonation preservation. Both are already integrated into LiveKit, Agora, and Pipecat. For builders targeting multilingual call centers, live event captioning, or cross-border support bots: the build-vs-buy math flipped this week. $0.023/min with zero manual language routing is below the engineering cost of maintaining a multilingual STT+MT+TTS pipeline — and the quality bar (intonation preserved, speaker pacing matched) is already above what most teams ship. The strategic question shifts to: what can you build *on top of* the translation layer that the raw API doesn't handle — accent normalization, domain-specific term retention, compliance logging, routing logic.

**Open-source is now closing the gap in STT, not just TTS.** For three weeks, the open-source pressure in voice AI was concentrated in TTS (Higgs Audio v3, Miso One, MOSS-TTS-Nano). Nemotron 3.5 ASR extends the pattern upstream: a self-hostable, fine-tunable streaming STT across 40 languages, from a vendor (NVIDIA) with the hardware distribution to make it fast by default. The fine-tuning pipeline is the moat claim — builders can tune Nemotron to their domain's vocabulary and accent profile in ways that commercial APIs with fixed endpoints don't allow. The 224-bookmark engagement is the tell: developers are saving this to build with, not just to read. The structural implication compounds with the TTS trend: within 18 months, "proprietary STT or TTS model" is no longer a defensible product axis. The compressible margin layer is the model; the defensible layer is what sits around it — session state, turn detection, recovery logic, and domain-specific tuning pipelines.
