## Where the puck is going

**The voice AI market is forking on a fundamental architectural question: proprietary models vs. orchestration.** Bland's $50M raise — on the strength of in-house models that customers cannot swap out — is the clearest capital-market endorsement of the vertical-integration thesis this cycle. It's a direct bet against the prevailing "best-of-breed pipeline" approach where platforms like Vapi (62M monthly calls, 14+ providers) and Retell let developers mix Deepgram STT, any LLM, and Cartesia TTS. Bland's counter-argument: in regulated industries where accuracy and continuity are non-negotiable, you can't afford the integration seams between third-party models. You need end-to-end control. That resonates — Bland's 175M+ calls last year and 250+ enterprise customers prove the model works at scale. But it's notable that ElevenLabs CTO Dąbkowski personally invested in Bland despite ElevenLabs pursuing the opposite strategy (wrapping around any LLM via Speech Engine). The sophisticated read: both approaches win, but in different segments. Orchestration wins developer volume and horizontal use cases; proprietary integration wins regulated verticals with high-stakes conversations. The market is big enough for both, and the buying criteria diverge enough that they're not actually competing head-to-head.

**The efficiency thesis for voice AI is crystallizing.** Smallest AI's claim that a 30B model matches ~600B systems on voice-agent benchmarks — if validated — is the clearest articulation of a trend running through several recent signals: purpose-built small models outperforming general-purpose giants on voice-specific tasks. This connects to the broader "right-sizing" pattern in the LLM market, but has specific implications for voice: smaller models mean lower inference cost, faster time-to-first-byte, and viable self-hosting on modest hardware. For builders, the question shifts from "which frontier model should I use?" to "what's the minimum model size that clears my quality bar for this specific voice use case?" The NVIDIA Nemotron 3.5 ASR release (600M params, 40 languages, June 11) pushed the same thesis from the STT side. Open-source and efficiency-first approaches are compressing the cost floor across the entire voice pipeline.

## Signals from the field

- **Developer (X, June 17):** *"Voice AI has a ~500ms latency budget. WebSocket pipelines burn most of it before your STT even starts."* — A builder arguing that WebRTC (UDP/RTP) vs. WebSocket (TCP) transport choice is the hidden latency variable that no vendor pricing page addresses. A missing 20ms audio frame is imperceptible; a 200ms TCP stall is not. Transport protocol is becoming the next named optimization layer below model-level latency.
- **Community (X, June 17):** *"PW choosing ElevenLabs over Sarvam proves that when it comes to low-latency voice models, India's premier Indic AI startup still can't compete."* — Referring to India's PhysicsWallah (edtech) reportedly picking ElevenLabs over locally-funded Sarvam AI ($234M for Indic languages). If accurate, it signals that quality and latency are trumping regional data advantage in enterprise procurement, even in markets where local-language coverage should be a structural advantage.
- **Deepgram (X, June 16):** At HLTH Europe, noting healthcare buyer shift from "should we?" to "how do we deploy compliantly?" — demand for self-hosted, EU-endpoint deployments is the specific requirement. The Fortanix confidential-computing partnership now has a named buyer cohort.
