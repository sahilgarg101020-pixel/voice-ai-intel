# Voice AI Brief — 2026-07-02

*A quiet post-AIEWF cycle, but three items worth a founder's attention: xAI verticalizes into the builder layer, Cartesia completes its session stack with telephony, and AssemblyAI becomes the first vendor to raise prices against the industry's cost collapse.*

## TLDR

- **xAI launches Grok Voice Agent Builder (beta) — a no-code voice agent platform direct from the model vendor** — Build and deploy a production voice agent on Grok Voice in "under two minutes," with telephony, tools, retrieval, guardrails, and observability bundled in. Pricing: $0.05/min agent audio + $0.01/min telephony, with free number provisioning — a 10-minute support call runs ~$0.60, undercutting ElevenLabs and Vapi on cost. 25+ languages, 80+ voices, sub-second latency claimed. ([x.ai](https://x.ai/news/grok-voice-agent-builder))

- **Cartesia adds SIP trunking (beta) and phone-number/provider APIs, completing its full session stack** — Sonic (TTS) + Ink-2 (STT) + Line (orchestration) now gets a telephony layer: bring-your-own-Twilio import or free Cartesia-provisioned numbers, all via API. Closes the last gap versus ElevenLabs' ElevenAgents telephony stack. ([Cartesia Changelog](https://docs.cartesia.ai/changelog/2026))

- **AssemblyAI raises in-region model pricing 10%, "due to provider cost increases"** — Effective July 1; customers can opt back into prior pricing by setting `model_region: "global"`. First documented price *increase* in this brief's entire tracked history, against a backdrop of Soniox ($2/1K min), Gemini Live ($0.00165/min), and OpenAI Realtime (~$0.04/min) all pushing prices down. ([AssemblyAI](https://www.assemblyai.com/pricing))

## Where the puck is going

**Model vendors are verticalizing straight into the builder layer, not just the API layer.** CCW two weeks ago was about CX platforms (Zoom, RingCentral, Talkdesk) shipping no-code voice agent builders for operators. xAI's Voice Agent Builder is the same move made by a *foundation model vendor* — skipping the orchestration middleman (Vapi, Retell, Bland) entirely and selling the finished agent-building experience directly, at aggressive per-minute pricing. This is the sharpest version yet of the "wrapper layer compression" risk this brief has tracked since June 19: it's no longer just Anthropic and Apple absorbing voice as a first-party modality — now xAI is competing directly with the orchestration platforms that built businesses on top of Grok Voice.

**The session-stack race just added a telephony front, and it's converging fast.** Cartesia entering SIP trunking mirrors ElevenLabs' ElevenAgents telephony move from three weeks ago almost exactly — both leading session-stack vendors are now absorbing the phone layer into their platforms rather than leaving developers to duct-tape Twilio on top. This matters because telephony/SIP costs were flagged back on June 9 as a hidden production COGS line invisible on every vendor pricing page; vendors bundling it in are effectively productizing a cost that used to be the builder's problem to solve.

**AssemblyAI's price increase is a small number with a bigger implication.** Every STT/TTS pricing story this brief has covered for two months has been a race to the bottom. A 10% in-region increase — with an explicit opt-out to "global" processing at the old price — suggests data-residency/in-region processing has real, rising infra cost that doesn't ride the same commodity curve as generic inference. Worth watching whether other vendors serving the sovereignty/compliance segment (Deepgram's confidential-computing stack, Gladia post-OVH) follow with their own regional pricing tiers — compliance-adjacent voice infra may be the one segment where the pricing curve bends up, not down.
