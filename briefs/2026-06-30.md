## Where the puck is going

**Turn detection is graduating from black-box model to precision-tunable API.** Three shipping features in the past two weeks tell the same story: ElevenLabs' `interruption_ignore_terms` (phrase-level suppression), Deepgram's `UpdateListen` (mid-session EOT confidence threshold), and Microsoft Voice Live's `threshold_level`/`timeout_ms` parameters. What was previously an opaque "latency vs. interruption" dial inside a model is becoming a documented API surface builders can tune per call stage, content type, and noise environment. The clinic operator data from June 12 (22-point CSAT jump from fixing false interruption, vs. nothing from three weeks of WER optimization) proved this was the right lever. Vendors are now shipping the controls. For builders: add false-interruption rate per content type to your eval suite — it's the next production quality axis to matter.

**The S2S cost floor has collapsed faster than anyone benchmarked.** At ~$0.04/min, OpenAI Realtime is now within striking distance of the component cost of a typical cascaded STT+LLM+TTS pipeline for medium call volumes. The economic argument against S2S — "too expensive for high-volume" — is weakening fast, even before Jalapeño ships more inference cost reduction by end of 2026. Gemini Live ($0.00165/min) is already an order of magnitude cheaper if latency and controllability allow. For builders still on cascaded: the question is no longer "can we afford S2S" but "what do we give up in control and debuggability when we switch."

**The developer signal check-in is happening right now at AIEWF.** CCW last week was the CX operator and platform buyer community (Zoom, RingCentral, Talkdesk, Salesforce). AIEWF this week is the 6,000-engineer developer community (ElevenLabs, Cartesia, Pipecat, LiveKit). Both communities are buying, but they're buying different things — platforms for operators, APIs and orchestration for developers. When the AIEWF program explicitly notes AI Agents are "front and center" and RAG/prompt engineering is being "pushed to the margins," that's a developer consensus signal worth tracking: the moat is orchestration and reliability, not raw model quality.
